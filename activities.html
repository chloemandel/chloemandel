Professional




Jobs & Internships





Architectural Designs, Wilton, CT:




I have been working at Architectural Designs since the summer following my sophomore year. I work two afternoons a week during the school year and throughout my summer breaks. I use Photoshop to color floor plans and I regularly update the company's website with both text and images. Through this internship, I've had the opportunity to expand my computer skills through real world business applications.









Today's Industrial Living Landscapes:




My sophomore English teacher recommended me for my internship at TILL, a non-profit focused on environmentally sustainable brownfield regeneration and technological advances within the Fairfield County community. For the past two years, I've worked with Fordham student Olivia Greenspan and New School professor Jane Philbrick to plan the regeneration and development of the Gilbert and Bennett Wire Mill, a former wire factory turned derelict brownfield site in Georgetown, CT. Our vision for the site includes a vertical farm, in which plants are grown vertically instead of horizontally to save land and preserve water. The plan also envisions a senior living center and a creative space for artists from nearby cities to work. Through my internship with TILL, I have also had the opportunity to research the development and testing of autonomous vehicles in Connecticut. As a culmination of this research, I moderated a panel on the subject with my state senator Toni Boucher in attendance. Additionally, I was invited to speak at a community-wide event discussing sustainable textiles, and I pitched TILL's plan for the wire mill to local businesses.

















Warrior Words:




Warrior Words is a student run column in my town's newspaper, the Wilton Bulletin. Six Wilton High School seniors are chosen by the paper to write articles on topics ranging from living in Wilton, to student life, to human interest stories. I applied for a columnist position in my junior year and was accepted.

Clubs & Extracurriculars

I am an editor for my school's newspaper, The Forum. We publish articles online and in print on subjects varying from school events to movie critiques and other opinion pieces. As editor, I am also responsible for uploading the articles onto the website, web maintenance, and advertising.








French Honors Society:




I was recommended for the French Honors Society by my teacher and am now the secretary of the club. I am responsible for organizing and attending bake sales, keeping track of club attendance, assigning tasks to younger club members, and tutoring younger high school students in French language and grammar.

Though I exercise regularly at my local gym, I wanted to have the experience of being on a real high school team. I decided to join the golf team in my sophomore year, as my grandparents and other extended family members are avid players and I had always wanted to learn. I have greatly improved my playing skills over the past few years, and have had lots of fun while doing so!









Wilton High School Orchestra:




I've been playing violin for eight years, and was incredibly excited to join my high school's orchestra when I returned from Europe. This past year, the orchestra received a superior ranking at the Spirit of America Music Festival in Baltimore, Maryland. I love playing violin, and I hope to continue playing in an orchestra in college.








Volunteer Work






Horizons at Greens Farms Academy:




I volunteer on Saturdays at Horizons, an academic enrichment program for elementary and middle school students from underserved schools in Bridgeport, CT and the surrounding area. I am responsible for helping students with their reading and math assignments, and leading group activities like games and sports. I chose to volunteer at Horizons because I enjoy tutoring, and I especially love helping students reach their academic potential.









Camp Invention




This past summer, I volunteered as a Leadership Intern at Camp Invention, a program for children ages 6 - 12 interested in STEM. I helped campers with several tasks including configuring light sensor robots and understanding binary code. I was also responsible for setting up camp supplies every morning and leading campers in games and activities.
