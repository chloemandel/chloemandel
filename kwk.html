Coding













How I Learned to Code


My first experience with computer science took place at a weekly beginner coding class at my local library. Though I enjoyed learning the basics there, I was disappointed to find that I was the only girl in the class. Furthermore, the curriculum came from Khan Academy which, though a well-regarded website, did little to teach me how to write code from scratch. After searching for computer science programs online, I found Kode With Klossy, a competitive two-week summer program for girls ages 13-18. Karlie Kloss, its founder, developed an interest in coding in 2014 and started Kode With Klossy in order to draw more young women into tech fields. I eagerly applied in the spring of 2016, submitting an essay and video. My acceptance letter, to my great surprise, arrived a few months later. That summer, I learned the fundamentals of Ruby, HTML, and CSS, building and presenting to the class my first website. In addition, I was introduced to a community of incredible girls who are just as passionate about coding as I am.
